My story

I'm Ayzal, born and raised in Lahore, Pakistan. Urdu has been part of every important moment in my life — family gatherings, poetry evenings, and everyday conversation. A few years ago I started helping a friend's partner learn Urdu before their wedding, and that one favor turned into a full teaching practice for international students.

Since then I've taught students preparing to meet their partner's family, students relocating for work, and students who simply fell in love with the language through music and film.

Experience

2020 – Present

Teaching Urdu online full-time to students across the UK, USA, Canada, and Europe.

2024 – 2025

Worked as a language instructor at The Educators, teaching Urdu as a second language.

2025

Began informally tutoring friends and family members of foreign students.

Qualifications

  • Bachelors' in Urdu Literature, Punjab University of Lahore
  • Certificate of Teaching Workshop (Age 3-12)
  • 6 years of dedicated 1-on-1 online teaching experience
  • Diploma for teaching Urdu to Adults

Teaching philosophy

I believe language learning should feel like a conversation with a friend, not a classroom lecture. Every student learns differently — some want to focus on speaking, others on reading or grammar — so I build each lesson plan around your real goals, not a rigid syllabus. Mistakes are welcome here. They're part of learning, not something to be afraid of.
